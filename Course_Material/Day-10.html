HTML semantics refers to the practice of using HTML elements in a way that reflects the meaning and structure of the content they enclose. In simpler terms, it's about choosing the appropriate HTML tags to describe the different parts of your webpage in a meaningful and logical manner. Here's a breakdown of HTML semantics: 1. **Meaningful Structure**: HTML provides a variety of elements to represent different types of content on a webpage, such as headings, paragraphs, lists, images, forms, etc. Using these elements appropriately ensures that the structure of your document is clear and understandable to both humans and machines (like search engines). 2. **Semantic Tags**: HTML5 introduced several new semantic elements that allow developers to more accurately describe the structure and purpose of their content.
